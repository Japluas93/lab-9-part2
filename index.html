Perseverance has been found to be able to make as much oxygen on Mars as a small tree

Landing on

Mars

Since Rover's landing on Mars, Nasa has been conducting the Mars Oxygen In-Situ Resource Utilization Experiment, also referred to as MOXIE, which has allowed the space team to make oxygen from the planet's carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere and has seen a lot of success. MOXIE has been able to produce oxygen in a variety of atmospheric conditions during the planet's day and night, and through different Martian seasons.

Oxygen Production

During each run Moxie has been able to reach its target usually producing around six grams of oxygen per hour. This rate is very comparable to small tree on Earth. Right now this is the first demonstration using resources of another planet and transforming them chemically into something that can be used by humans.

Fun facts about Mars Perseverance Rover

The Mars Perseverance Rover is 10 feet long, 9 feet wide, and 7 feet tall.

The Mars Perseverance Rover weighs 2,260 pounds

The Mars Perseverance Rover is nuclear powered

The Mars Perseverance Rover touched down on the Jezero Crater on February 18th, 2021
